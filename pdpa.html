1. Introduction

Nintendo Co., Ltd. and its affiliated companies (collectively "Nintendo," "we," "our," or "us") respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you:

  • Use our Nintendo Switch Lite console and related services
  • Visit our websites, including purchasing products from our online stores
  • Use our mobile applications
  • Contact our customer service team
  • Subscribe to our marketing communications
  • Participate in our events, surveys, or promotions

This Privacy Policy is designed to comply with applicable data protection laws, including the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA). By using our products and services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

Important: Please read this Privacy Policy carefully to understand our policies and practices regarding your personal information and how we will treat it. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our products and services.

2. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information from and about users of our products and services:

2.1 Personal Information You Provide to Us

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you:

  • Create a Nintendo Account
  • Purchase and register products
  • Contact customer support
  • Participate in surveys or promotions
  • Post content in our online communities
  • Submit job applications

This information may include:

  • Contact information (name, email address, postal address, telephone number)
  • Account information (user name, password, security questions)
  • Demographic information (date of birth, gender, country of residence)
  • Payment information (credit card details, billing address)
  • Your preferences and interests
  • Communications you send us

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use our products and services, we may collect certain information automatically, including:

  • Device information (device identifiers, IP address, operating system)
  • Network information (Wi-Fi MAC address, Bluetooth address)
  • Console-specific information (Nintendo Switch Lite serial number)
  • Usage data (games played, play duration, interaction with features)
  • Location information (region settings, time zone)
  • Online activity data collected through cookies and similar technologies

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, including:

  • Social media platforms when you connect them to your Nintendo Account
  • Game developers and publishers who partner with Nintendo
  • Marketing partners and analytics providers
  • Payment processors and financial institutions

4. Sharing Your Information

We may share your personal information with the following categories of recipients:

4.1 Nintendo Group Companies

We may share your information with our parent company, subsidiaries, and affiliates to provide integrated services and for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy.

4.2 Service Providers

We engage trusted third-party service providers to perform functions on our behalf, such as:

  • Payment processing
  • Customer support
  • Cloud hosting and storage
  • Analytics and research
  • Marketing and advertising
  • Product shipping and fulfillment

These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and may only use it for the specific purposes we have designated.

4.3 Business Partners

4.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information when we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to:

  • Comply with applicable laws, regulations, or legal process
  • Protect the safety, rights, or property of Nintendo, our users, or others
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud or security issues
  • Enforce our terms of service and other agreements

4.5 Business Transfers

If Nintendo is involved in a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any change in ownership or uses of your personal information.

Note: We do not sell your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

5. Data Retention and Security

5.1 Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. The criteria used to determine our retention periods include:

  • The length of time we have an ongoing relationship with you
  • Whether there is a legal obligation to which we are subject
  • Whether retention is advisable considering our legal position (such as for statutes of limitations, litigation, or regulatory investigations)

When personal information is no longer needed, we will securely delete or anonymize it.

5.2 Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data
  • Secure network configurations
  • Access controls and authentication procedures
  • Regular security assessments and testing
  • Staff training on data protection practices
  • Physical security measures at our facilities

While we strive to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but continuously work to enhance our security measures.

Security Tips: We recommend that you protect your Nintendo Account with a strong password, enable two-factor authentication, and never share your login credentials with others.

6. Your Rights Under PDPA

Under the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), you have certain rights regarding your personal information. These include:

6.1 Right to Access

You have the right to request access to your personal information that we hold and to obtain information about how we process it.

6.2 Right to Correction

You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you.

6.3 Right to Restriction

In certain circumstances, you have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information.

6.4 Right to Data Portability

You may have the right to receive a copy of your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller.

6.5 Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, including for direct marketing purposes.

6.6 Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your personal information based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on your consent before its withdrawal.

6.7 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in the "Contact Us" section. We will respond to your request within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

Please note that some of these rights may be limited where we have compelling legitimate grounds or legal obligations to continue processing your personal information.

7. Children's Privacy

We recognize the importance of protecting children's privacy and take specific measures to protect children who use our products and services:

7.1 Age Requirements

Nintendo Switch Online services are intended for users of all ages, but certain features have age restrictions:

  • Children under 13 (or the applicable minimum age in your country) need parental consent to create a Nintendo Account
  • Parents/guardians must create and manage accounts for younger children through Nintendo's Parental Controls system

7.2 Information Collection from Children

When we knowingly collect personal information from children, we:

  • Obtain verifiable parental consent before collection
  • Limit the information collected to what is reasonably necessary
  • Provide parents with access to review and manage their child's information
  • Implement additional security measures to protect children's data

7.3 Parental Controls

Nintendo Switch Lite offers robust parental controls that allow parents/guardians to:

  • Manage their child's friend interactions
  • Restrict online communication features
  • Limit eShop purchases
  • Set playtime limits
  • Control access to age-restricted content

For more information about our parental controls, please visit https://www.nintendo.com/switch/parental-controls/.

8. International Data Transfers

Nintendo operates globally, which means your personal information may be transferred to, stored, and processed in countries outside your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your country.

When we transfer your personal information internationally, we implement appropriate safeguards to ensure that your information remains protected in accordance with this Privacy Policy and applicable law. These safeguards may include:

  • Standard contractual clauses approved by relevant data protection authorities
  • Intra-group data transfer agreements
  • Verification that recipients are subject to laws providing adequate protection
  • Obtaining your explicit consent for specific transfers where required

By using our products and services, you acknowledge that your personal information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence.

9. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience, analyze usage, and assist in our marketing efforts.

9.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently and provide information to the website owners.

9.2 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential cookies: Required for our websites and services to function properly
  • Functional cookies: Allow us to remember your preferences and customize your experience
  • Analytical cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our websites
  • Advertising cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements and track campaign performance

9.3 Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we may use:

  • Web beacons: Small graphic images that allow us to monitor website usage
  • Local storage: Stores data locally on your device for technical purposes
  • Software development kits (SDKs): In mobile applications to collect information about how the apps are used

9.4 Your Cookie Choices

You can manage your cookie preferences through:

  • Our cookie preference settings on our websites
  • Your browser settings (to block or delete cookies)
  • Third-party opt-out tools for advertising cookies

Please note that blocking certain cookies may impact the functionality of our websites and services.

10. Updates to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes to this Privacy Policy, we will:

  • Post the updated Privacy Policy on our websites
  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of the Privacy Policy
  • Provide notice through our services or by other means as required by applicable law

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about our information practices. Your continued use of our products and services after any changes to this Privacy Policy constitutes your acceptance of the changes.
